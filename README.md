# Inventory-Intelligence-Hub-in-Powerbi

A business intelligence dashboard built with **Microsoft Power BI** to help retail businesses monitor inventory, analyze sales performance, and make data-driven decisions. This project transforms raw inventory data into meaningful insights through interactive visualizations and key performance indicators (KPIs).

---

## 📖 Overview

Managing inventory across multiple retail stores can be challenging when data is spread across different systems and reports. Decision-makers need quick access to accurate information to ensure products are available at the right place and the right time.

The **Inventory Intelligence Hub** provides a centralized dashboard that enables users to monitor inventory levels, track sales performance, compare store performance, and analyze products from multiple perspectives. The dashboard is designed to simplify reporting and support faster, data-driven business decisions.

---

## 💼 Business Problem

Retail businesses often struggle to gain a complete view of their inventory because information is stored across multiple reports or systems. This makes it difficult to answer important business questions such as:

- Which stores have the highest or lowest inventory?
- Which products are selling well, and which are slow-moving?
- Are there products that need to be replenished or redistributed?
- Which product categories contribute the most to inventory?
- How does sales performance vary across different stores?

Without a centralized reporting system, businesses may experience stock shortages, excess inventory, delayed decision-making, and inefficient stock management.

---

## 💡 Solution

The **Inventory Intelligence Hub** addresses these challenges by providing a single, interactive Power BI dashboard that consolidates inventory and sales data into meaningful business insights.

The dashboard allows users to:

- Monitor total stock quantity, sold quantity, and inventory value.
- Compare inventory across multiple store locations.
- Analyze inventory by category, style, color, size, and gender.
- Track sales performance for each store.
- Filter data by season, designer, category, label, and product style.
- Identify inventory trends and support replenishment decisions.

By presenting business-critical information in one place, the dashboard helps improve operational efficiency and enables better inventory planning.
